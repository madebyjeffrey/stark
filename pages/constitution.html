Constitution of Student Operated Computing Resources

Approved on 2013-11-01

  1. Name

    The name of this organization shall be Student Operated Computing Resources or SOCR.

  2. Object

    The object of SOCR shall be to provide computing resources to the members as defined in section 3.1.

  3. Members
    1. Regular Members

      Regular members shall consist of all University of Windsor students registered in any degree program offered by the University and has paid the allotted fee to SOCR.

      1. Status of Membership

        A member is in good standing during a given semester if said member has paid the applicable fee to SOCR for that semester and satisfies any other requirements as set forth in this instrument.

      2. Rights and Privileges of Members

        A member in good standing shall be permitted to use the services provided by SOCR within the limitations described here and in referenced policies.

        Additionally, said member shall be able to request access to any SOCR records that are not classified confidential by the Board.

      3. Membership in the Board

        A regular member in good standing shall be eligible to hold any position on the Board and be eligible to vote in referendums and general elections.

    2. Honourary Members

      An honourary member is a member, who is not subject to the dues of regular members, but is afforded only the rights and privileges as described in section 3.1.2.

      1. Conferred

        The president or CTO may confer, upon request, an honourary membership upon an individual for a period of no more than two years. This membership is subject to review of the board.

      2. Term

        An honourary member shall retain membership for a period of no more than two years at a time unless renewed in accord with section 3.2.1. If this period is between two meetings, it shall be extended to the end of the next meeting.

      3. Renewal

        A current or past honourary member may request renewal of membership, by request to the president or CTO, subject to review of the board.

  4. Officers
    1. Positions

      The officers of SOCR shall be President, Vice President, Treasurer, Chief Technical Officer, and three general members.

    2. The Board

      The officers of SOCR shall collectively be known as the Board.

    3. Eligibility

      All elected positions shall be filled by a regular member.

    4. Non-Elected Positions

      The Chief Technical Officer shall be a qualified person appointed by the board, until a successor is appointed.

    5. Duties

      These officers shall perform the duties prescribed by these bylaws and by the parliamentary authority adopted by SOCR.

    6. President

      The President shall be the official representative of SOCR, notwithstanding the authority granted to other persons.

    7. Chief Technical Officer

      The Chief Technical Officer shall provide software and hardware support as outlined by the SOCR Operating Policy.

    8. Vice President

      The Vice President shall provide oversight of operations, including but not limited to recording of minutes and documentation of operations.

    9. Treasurer

      The Treasurer shall be responsible for all financial interests of SOCR.

    10. General Members

      The General Members shall delegate other responsibilities as needed.

  5. Board
    1. Composition

      The elected officers, appointed officers, and general board members shall constitute the Board.

    2. Duties and Powers

      The Board shall have general supervision of the affairs of SOCR between its business meetings, fix the hour and place of meetings, make recommendations to the members, and perform such other duties as are specified in these by-laws.

    3. Meetings

      The meetings of the Board shall be held as needed, on a date determined by the president or two members of the board. Notice of two days is required, and the caller of the meeting shall make a best effort to accomodate all parties.

    4. Quorum

      Quorum shall consist of one half of the members of the Board rounded up. The number of members taken as the total shall be counting the people, not positions.

    5. Lack of Quorum

      If a quorum is not reached, then no decisions can be made that require Board approval. Any other business regarding reports and new motions can be presented, and must be evaluated at the next meeting. A time for a new meeting can be chosen by the members present.

    6. Open to Members

      All meetings of SOCR shall be open to regular members, but no voting rights will be extended to regular members.

    7. Questions from Members

      At any meeting, the Chairperson shall provide for regular members to be afforded a time to ask relevant questions of Board members.

    8. Voting Rights

      At all meetings convened by the Board, members of the Board have voting rights.

    9. One vote per Member

      Where a member holds more than one voting position, that member may only vote once and shall have the same weight as any other vote.

    10. Conflicts of Interest

      If a member of the Board is also a member of another organization with competing interests, that member must abstain from voting where that member or the President determines a conflict of interest. A member of the Board with a conflict of interest shall still count towards quorum.

    11. Breaking of Ties

      In the event of a vote resulting in a tie, the President must resolve the tie. If the President has cast a vote, the President shall retract the vote, otherwise the President may cast a tie breaking vote.

    12. Removal

      The board may, by a two thirds majority, remove a board member for failure to perform assigned duties, or misconduct in performing those duties.

    13. Resignation

      A board member may resign from their position by notifying the board or president.

    14. Filling a Vacant Position

      The President shall be empowered to filling vacant positions, by appointment or election, as the President deems necessary, subject to approval by the board.

    15. Resignation of the President

      In the case of the resignation of the President, the position shall be filled by the Treasurer until the next election.

  6. Technical Services

    The services provided by SOCR shall be administered and defined in accord with the SOCR Operating Policy and administered by the Chief Technical Officer.

    1. Administrators

      The Chief Technical Officer shall appoint administrators and delegate responsibilities.

    2. Representative

      The Chief Technical Officer shall designate a proxy to be the representative at meetings, where the Chief Technical Officer is unable to attend.

    3. Modification

      The SOCR Operating Policy may be modified by motion of the Board or on the recommendation of the Chief Technical Officer.

    4. Limitations

      The services provided are subject to the policies of the University of Windsor and the IT Services department of same.

    5. Liability

      The members may not use the service in a way that creates liability for SOCR, the University of Windsor, or the IT Services department of same.

  7. Financial

    All financial transactions must be authorized by two of the President, Treasurer, and Vice President.

  8. Disciplinary Action

    Any member who violates SOCR Operating Policy, any other governing document, or any other offense acknowledged by the discretion of the board may be subject to sanctions, up to and including termination of membership.

    1. Security

      The Chief Technical Officer's first concern is the security and integrity of the services provided, and is authorized to take appropriate steps to ensure that security.

    2. Notification

      When a violation is occurring, or there exists a good faith belief of such violation, the member(s) involved shall be notified of the violation and warranted actions.

    3. Further Action

      Where further action is required, the Chief Technical Officer shall take the appropriate action after notifying the board.

    4. Redress

      Where the member feels need of redress, the President or a general board member can be contacted to request board intervention.

    5. Suspension

      Where a severe violation has occurred, the board may suspend membership of the member.

    6. Remedies

      Where the society has incurred monetary liability, the member shall assume these liabilities.

  9. Committees

    The board may designate committees as needed.

  10. Elections

    The board or President shall call elections as needed, by appointing a Chief Returning Officer to coordinate with the University of Windsor Students' Alliance (UWSA) election representative.

    1. Annual Elections

      An annual election in the Winter semester is to be normally carried out, with offices filled in May.

    2. Elected Positions

      The elected positions of SOCR shall be President, Treasurer, Vice President, and three general members.

    3. Vacant Positions

      The President is empowered, subject to approval of the Board, to appoint any positions not filled in an election. These positions are held until the next election. If the vacant positions are from the Annual Elections, if possible the followup election must be in the fall.

    4. Continuity

      The outgoing Board shall coordinate with the new Board to ensure a smooth transition. Appointed positions shall be retained until new appointees are selected.

    5. Chief Returning Officer

      The Chief Returning Officer (CRO) shall oversee all SOCR elections, and be responsible for setting an election policy.

    6. Eligible

      All regular members of SOCR are eligible to run for a position and vote.

    7. Regulations

      The CRO shall publish an elections policy and timeline substantially inline with the UWSA Elections Policy.

  11. Parliamentary Authority

    The rules contained in the current edition of Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised shall govern SOCR in all cases to which they are applicable and in which they are not inconsistent with these bylaws and any special rules of order SOCR may adopt.

  12. Amendmnets

    These bylaws may be amended at any Board Meeting of SOCR by a simple majority vote, provided the amendment has been submitted in writing to the Board a week prior to the meeting.

  13. Interpretation of Bylaws

    These bylaws are to be interpreted by the Board.

  14. Conflicts with UWSA Policy

    Nothing in this instrument shall be read to contradict UWSA rules regarding clubs. In the event where a contradiction shall occur, the rules of the UWSA shall have precedent.
