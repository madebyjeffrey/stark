SOCR Election 2013

At the meeting of 19 Oct 2013, the election was called and Jeffrey Drake was appointed as Chief Returning Officer. All questions about the election should be directed to him.

Note: This website shall be updated as the need arises.

  • Nomination Form

Election Schedule

  • Nominations: November 4th to 8th (Monday to Friday)
  • All Candidates Meeting: November 8th at 6pm in Council Chambers
  • Campaign: November 9th to 17th
  • Voting Day: November 18th
  • Last day for Appeals: November 21st at 6pm

Election Rules and Procedure

  1. Authority: This policy is set by the CRO under section 10.5 of the constitution.
  2. Maintenance: The CRO (Jeffrey Drake) coordinates the election proceedings, while the VPA UWSA (Shaista Akbar) sets up the election site. Both CRO and VPA UWSA do not run in the election.
  3. The Election shall follow the schedule listed above.
  4. All nominees must attend the All Candidates Meeting to maintain candidacy. If the nominee cannot attend this meeting they must send a proxy and inform the CRO prior to 6pm on November 8th.
  5. The nomination forms must be filled in their entirety and brought to the UWSA Office on the second floor of the CAW building.
  6. A Nomination Form will not be invalid as a result of a failure by any Officer or employee of the UWSA or SOCR.
  7. Nominees who comply with section 4, shall become candidates.
  8. If a candidate is running uncontested, that candidate shall be acclaimed, subject to appeals.
  9. Campaign materials must follow all applicable rules from the UWSA Election Policies. Violation of these policies may result in sanctions.
  10. No remuneration for election materials will be made.
  11. During the voting period, Part 7.1 and 7.1.1 of the UWSA Election Policies shall be followed.
  12. Voting shall occur on voting day from 9am to 9pm. With unofficial results the following day at noon.
  13. Appeals shall be heard by the CRO. Submission must occur via email at drakej@uwindsor.ca.
  14. Appeals must occur by the last day for appeals.
  15. Appeals must consist of specific grounds for appeal, indicating that due process was not followed, important evidence was missing, or the decision was inconsistent with the principles of natural justice.
  16. Appeals may be heard by a committee of three members consisting of the CRO and two others chosen by the CRO where a conflict of interest does not exist.
